## Development judgment

- Vicia preserved exact data and recovered its acknowledged WAL prefix after
  kill-9. Stability is not the comparative weakness in this run.
- Vicia point reads remained fast, but accumulated reopen, full-scan
  materialization, peak RSS, and storage amplification are the next measured
  development boundaries.
- Vicia's full scan materialized one million Datalog result rows. The reported
  2.05 GiB is the process-wide high-water mark across build/checkpoint and all
  later phases, not a scan-only measurement; it is not evidence that ordinary
  indexed Vetch reads use 2 GiB. Phase-isolated memory requires a fresh process.
- Vicia stores bi-temporal identity plus four graph indexes. Cozo's fixture is a
  current EAV relation, SQLite is a hand-shaped current table, and redb removes
  query/graph/temporal semantics. Their smaller byte counts are useful cost
  floors, not proof that those engines provide equivalent behavior.
- The 8.513 ms Vicia append p95 is below the current 10 ms Vetch cadence budget
  but close enough that weekly variance and the dedicated M2 gate should be
  watched before storage work claims headroom.

Do not compare these values with another host unless the exact profile, source,
durability settings, and host provenance match.
